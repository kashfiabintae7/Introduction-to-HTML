Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is a biological process that converts light energy into chemical energy. It occurs in plants, algae, and some types of bacteria.

How It Works:

1. Light-dependent reactions

Chlorophyll in the thylakoid membrane absorbs light energy from sunlight. This energy is converted into chemical energy in the form of ATP and NADPH molecules.

2. Light-independent reactions

The ATP and NADPH molecules produced in the light-dependent reactions are used in the light-independent reactions.

3. Calvin cycle

Plants and algae use the Calvin cycle to convert carbon dioxide from the air into sugar.

Importance

Photosynthesis is essential for life on Earth because it provides energy and food for plants and animals.
